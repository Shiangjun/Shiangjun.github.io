I have been a PhD student at CSE@CUHK since 2022 fall. My advisor is Prof. Ming-Chang Yang. Before that, I earned my MPhil from CUHK (in 2022) and Honors BSc from UNNC (in 2020), all in Computer Science (and Engineering).

Interests: My current interests lie on architecture-system co-designs for Near-Memory Acceleration. The data movement bottleneck has been proven as one of the major obstacles for efficient computing. systems in the past two decades, and the emerging trends of Near-Memory Computing bring new challenges for the whole computing stack. My works includes methods and systems to improve the programmability, efficiency [Under Submission] and security [In Progress]. We are also synthesizing and extending our ideas in an unified prototype, to make them more portable and accessible for different Near-Memory Accelerators.

Avocations: I generally enjoy working with bright individuals, who persist to acknowledge contributions and refuse the bureaucracy (from both ourselves and others), on whatever they think intellectually interesting. From 2018 to 2023, I founded, have served as the first Project Manager, and currently a graudate affiliate (now led by my friend Wangkai Jin) in the User-Centric Computing Group [The website is temporarily down, thanks to the ``leadership" of UNNC CS for identifying us as the ``unofficial" affiliation], an independent research soceity for promoting undergraduate research (for students at UNottingham Ningbo CS). Our five-year project strives for usable infrastructures and methods for Human-Vehicle Interactive designs [See Our Project Releases Here].
